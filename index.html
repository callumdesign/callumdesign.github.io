About Me

I studied at Victoria University, and completed a bachelor of design innovation majoring in industrial design in 2017. I have had three years of experience using CAD packages such as Solidworks and Rhinoceros to design for additive manufacturing technologies as well as other CNC machining techniques.

Some practical attributes I have include hands on experience operating consumer level 3D printers such as the UP! Box FDM machines, as well as designing parts to be made on printers such as the Stratasys J750 and Connex printers. I also have experience using rendering software such as Keyshot, to produce images and animations of prototypes to demonstrate features of a design.

Micro, Charge on the go!

This project was completed in cooperation with Micro Mobility and Victoria University of Wellington. The purpose of the project was to design & complimentary charging service for Micro customers. The service aims to provide Micro customers with an appealing and convenient method of charging electric scooters in order to encourage their widespread use. By doing so, Micro Charge on the Go builds on Micro Mobility's "world first" branding value to reinforce the idea that electric scooters are a useful and convenient urban transport option.
